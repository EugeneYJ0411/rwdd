Tips

Simple, actionable steps to reduce your energy consumption at home or work.

  • Tip 1: Use LED or energy-efficient lighting: LED bulbs use up to ~90% less energy than incandescent and last much longer.
  • Tip 2: Unplug devices (or use smart power strips) when not in use: even “standby” electronics draw power.
  • Tip 3: Improve insulation and seal air leaks: Draughts around windows, doors or ceilings waste heating/cooling energy.
  • Tip 4: Set a smart or programmable thermostat: Adjust heating/cooling based on occupancy or time of day to save energy.
  • Tip 5: Maximise use of natural daylight: Open curtains/blinds in daytime, keep artificial lights off where possible.
  • Tip 6: Choose high-efficiency appliances (look for Energy Star or equivalent): They cost more upfront, but save energy and money long-term.
  • Tip 7: Maintain HVAC and heating/cooling systems regularly: Clean filters, check ducts, service the system so it runs efficiently.
  • Tip 8: Use water heating wisely: Lower your water heater temperature, insulate the tank, run full loads of laundry/dishes.
  • Tip 9: In workplaces: Install motion sensors/timers for lighting and heating in unoccupied rooms or zones.
  • Tip 10: In workplaces: Encourage or enforce “power-down” policy for computers/printers after hours to avoid wasted energy.
